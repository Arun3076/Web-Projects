Pichai Sundararajan

June 10, 1972 (age 51)

CEO of Google and Alphabet

Sundar Pichai, the CEO of Alphabet Inc., stands as a symbol of visionary leadership and technological innovation. Born in Madurai, India, in 1972, Pichai's journey began with academic excellence at IIT Kharagpur, followed by advanced degrees from Stanford University and the Wharton School. Joining Google in 2004, he quickly rose through the ranks, leaving an indelible mark with his pivotal role in creating Google Chrome, the widely acclaimed web browser. Pichai's leadership extended to critical projects, including Android, before being appointed CEO of Google in 2015 and later becoming the CEO of Alphabet in 2019. His impact is not only evident in steering these tech giants but also in his advocacy for responsible AI use and commitment to digital literacy, making Sundar Pichai a transformative figure in the technology landscape.

Biography

  • Born on July 12, 1972, in Madurai, Tamil Nadu,India
  • Studied Metallurgical Engineering at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Kharagpur.
  • Pursued a Master's degree in Material Sciences and Engineering at Stanford University.
  • Earned an MBA from the Wharton School of the University of Pennsylvania.
  • Joined Google in 2004, initially working on the Google Toolbar.
  • Played a crucial role in the development of Google Chrome, a globally acclaimed web browser.
  • Led the Chrome and Apps division, overseeing products like Chrome OS, Gmail, Google Maps, and Google Drive.
  • Appointed CEO of Google in August 2015, succeeding Larry Page.
  • Became the CEO of Alphabet Inc. in December 2019, overseeing the broader conglomerate.
  • Transformed Google into a global tech giant and diversified Alphabet Inc.'s business portfolio.
